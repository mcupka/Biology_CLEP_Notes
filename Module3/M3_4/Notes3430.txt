3.4.3 Biomedical Progress

Nutrition
-We've learned the importance of essential nutrients, allowing healthier, longer lives. Decreases sickness due to malnutrition

Medical Advances
-Development of antibiotics in the 1920s decreased deaths from infection
-Vaccines protect from previously lethal diseases (flu, smallpox, rabies, etc)
-Antiviral treatments decrease sickness after viral infection (AIDS)

Treatments
-Improvements in managing illness, synthesizing molecules have allowed people to live longer and more comfortably
	-heart disease, osteoporosis, arthritis, cancer, diabetes, etc

Other Advances
-GMOs have increased agricultural output
-Use bacteria to make:
	-human insulin
	-vaccines
	-cancer treatments
-Investigating stem cells for organ transplantation
